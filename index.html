INF-PCA: Implicit Neural Field-based Interactive Point Cloud Semantic Annotation

Chong Liu1, Xu Han2, Chen Long1, Wang Wang1, Zhen Dong1, Bisheng Yang1,
1LIESMARS, Wuhan University 2Sensetime Research, Sensetime
Paper Code Data WHU-USI3DV

Abstract

Point cloud semantic segmentation is of great significance to 3D scene understanding, which relies on large amounts of labeled training data. Nevertheless, manually annotating large-scale datasets is quite time-consuming and tedious. This paper proposes an interactive point cloud semantic annotation method based on implicit neural field, which allows users to achieve high-quality, large-scene and multi-class semantic annotation with only a few dozen mouse clicks. Firstly, the appearance, geometry and semantic of the point clouds are jointly represented by an implicit neural field, which maps a 3D spatial coordinate to its corresponding attributes. Secondly, an uncertainty-based semantic entropy loss and a supervoxel-based local consistency loss are designed to force the network to produce deterministic predictions with local consistency and generate smoother and more accurate boundaries. Furthermore, an active learning-based strategy for click-free annotation is proposed and analyzed to further improve the annotation efficiency. Comprehensive experiments revealed that the proposed method reaches over 95% overall accuracy with less than 100 clicks per scene, and achieves more accurate annotations in indoor and outdoor scenes with less than 33% and 12% of clicks compared with the state-of-the-art method.

Overview

Workflow of the proposed interactive point cloud semantic annotation method INF-PCA: (a)offline reconstruction of the appearance field and the geometry field; (b)online interactive semantic annotation and semantic field reconstruction.


Firstly, the implicit neural field function F is off-line initialized by reconstructing the appearance field and the geometry field. Secondly, the users annotate a few points by mouse clicking, and the function F is optimized by jointly reconstructing the appearance field, the geometry field and the semantic field. Thirdly, the function F predicts the semantic labels of each point, the users choose misclassified points and correct their labels. Finally, the function F is further optimized based on the updated semantic supervision. The last two steps are sequentially iterated until the predicted semantic segmentation results reach a user-satisfactory accuracy.

Active learning-based click-free annotation

In the principle of interactive annotation, the users need to continuously select and modify misclassified points to optimize the segmentation model, which usually requires the users to frequently judge the segmentation results and manipulate the 3D visualization interface. To reduce the manual operation mentioned above and further reduce the cost of manual annotation, an active learning-based strategy for click-free annotation is proposed and analyzed in this section.
